Single-hidden-layer neural network:

Optimization objective: Refining the $IW$ (input-layer weights), such that the hidden feature $H$ gets refined.

  1. Iteration 1

    • Forward:

      $$ \begin{aligned} H = IW â‹… X \\\ Y_{pred} = \bm Î² â‹… H \end{aligned} $$

    • Compute error E:

      $$ E = T - Y_{pred} $$

    • Considering there is an imaginary data $P$ resulting in the error $E$ formulated by the equation: $E = \bm Î² â‹… P$

      Thus, the data $P$ can be solved by the pseudo-inverse of $\bm Î²$:

      $$ P = \bm Î²âº â‹… E $$

    • Using the $P$ to solve a “supplemental IW” $IW_{supp}$ by considering the relationship: $P = IW_{supp} â‹… X$

      $$ IW_{supp} = Xâº â‹… P $$

    • Update the input-layer weight by adding the supplemental $IW_{res}$ to the initial $IW$

      $$ IW = IW + IW_{supp} $$

    • Update $\bm Î²$ based on the updated $IW$ and the equation $T = \bm Î² â‹… H$:

      $$ \begin{aligned} H = IW â‹… X \\\ \bm Î² = Hâº â‹… T \end{aligned} $$

    • Compute the error at present:

      $$ \begin{aligned} Y_{pred} = \bm Î² â‹… H \\\ E = T - Y_{pred} \end{aligned} $$

  2. Iteration 2:

    • Compute $P$
    • Compute supplemental $IW_{res}$
    • Update $IW$
    • Update $Î²$
    • Compute $E$

  3. Iteration 3:

    • Perform the same 5 steps
